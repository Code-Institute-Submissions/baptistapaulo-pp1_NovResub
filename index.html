Brazilian Culture & Gastronomy


Brazil is a multicultural country so the foods traditinally eaten are a result of diverse people's ethnic backgrounds. The main ingredients of Brazilien diet are rice, beans and manioc. Meat, poultry and fish can then be added as desired. This way, the national dish is called "feijoada" and is comprised of several meats slowly cooked with black beans. Orange slices, rice, chopped collard greens and farinha can be mixed with the stew. On the other hand, for pastry, the "brigadeiros" are the version of chocolate truffles where sweet balls are made by simmering condensed milk with cocoa powder, then whisking in butter and shapping the mix into balls before rolling in chocolate sprinkles.

Portuguese Culture & Gastronomy


Portuguese colonism in the 14th and 15th centuries, built an empire in far regions explored including Brazil, Mozambique, Angol, Guine-Bissau, Cape Verde, Sao Tome, Principe, East Timor, Macau and Goa (India). The country is ethnically quite homogeneous and the first language, is the same in almost in all the (ex)colonies. The Atlantic coast provides a huge amount of fresh fish, seafood and dried salted cod so for that reason, the cod with potatos and vegetables is one of the most traditional national dishes but various kinds of meat are also common in the interior. Another falous dish is the "Cozido a Portuguesa" (a Portuguese stew). The waether and fertil soil, provides ideal conditions for wine making so the country is also well known by wine lovers and winemakers. Since the Roman Empire era, Portugal was associated by the romans with its God of wine (Bachus).
